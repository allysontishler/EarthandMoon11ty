Moon

The Moon, Earth's natural satellite, is much smaller than the Earth, with a diameter of about 3,474 kilometers. Composed mainly of silicate rock and dust, the Moon lacks a substantial atmosphere and experiences extreme temperature variations. It is thought to have formed around 4.5 billion years ago through a giant impact, where a Mars-sized body collided with the early Earth, ejecting debris that eventually formed into the Moon. As the Earth's constant companion, the Moon's gravitational influence plays a crucial role in stabilizing our planet's axial tilt and governing tides.
